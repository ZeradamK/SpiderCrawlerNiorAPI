# Regulatory Reporting

## Core Philosophy
**"File accurately, file on time — regulators judge you by your reporting as much as your controls."**

## Report Types

### Currency Transaction Report (CTR) — FinCEN Form 112
- **Trigger**: Cash transaction >$10,000 (or aggregated in one business day)
- **Filing deadline**: 15 calendar days from transaction date
- **Contents**: Customer info, transaction details, teller/branch info
- **Exemptions**: Banks, government agencies, NYSE-listed companies (with filed exemption)

### Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) — FinCEN Form 111
- **Trigger**: Known or suspected violation of law, >$5,000 involving a customer
- **Filing deadline**: 30 calendar days from initial detection (60 days if no suspect identified)
- **Contents**: Subject info, activity narrative, supporting documentation
- **No tipping off**: NEVER inform the subject of a SAR filing

### Report of International Transportation of Currency (CMIR) — FinCEN Form 105
- **Trigger**: Physical transport of >$10,000 in currency across US borders
- **Filing deadline**: At time of transport

### Report of Foreign Bank and Financial Accounts (FBAR) — FinCEN Form 114
- **Trigger**: US persons with foreign financial accounts >$10,000 aggregate
- **Filing deadline**: April 15 (with automatic extension to October 15)

## Filing Workflow

### SAR Filing Process
1. **Detection**: AML monitoring system flags suspicious activity
2. **Investigation**: Analyst reviews and documents findings (15 days)
3. **Draft**: Prepare SAR narrative and supporting data
4. **Review**: BSA Officer reviews and approves
5. **File**: Submit via FinCEN BSA E-Filing system
6. **Retain**: Store SAR and supporting documentation for 5 years
7. **Monitor**: Continue monitoring subject for 90 days post-filing
8. **Continuing activity**: File follow-up SAR if activity continues (within 90 days)

### CTR Filing Process
1. **Trigger**: Cash transaction exceeds $10,000
2. **Verify**: Customer identification (CIP)
3. **Complete**: FinCEN Form 112
4. **File**: Submit within 15 calendar days
5. **Retain**: Keep copy for 5 years

## Examination Readiness
- Maintain organized filing records
- Document all policies and procedures
- Track filing timeliness metrics
- Prepare management reports (filing counts, trends, quality)
- Conduct annual independent testing
